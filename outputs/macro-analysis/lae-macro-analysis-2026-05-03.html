Macro Assessment

The US macro landscape currently exhibits a predominantly Goldilocks regime. Inflation, as measured by CPI YoY at 3.49% and Core CPI YoY at 2.81%, remains elevated but shows signs of moderating, preventing a clear disinflationary trend but also avoiding overheating. These figures suggest a stable, though not accelerating, price environment.

Economic growth remains solid, with Real GDP QoQ at 2.00%. This growth is neither excessively strong to reignite runaway inflation nor weak enough to signal a downturn. This aligns with a scenario where the economy is expanding at a sustainable pace, consistent with the neutral inflation readings.

Labor market indicators are robust. The Unemployment Rate at 4.30% is low, and Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) show a healthy monthly change of 178.00, both pointing to continued job creation and economic vitality. These factors support consumer spending and overall economic expansion.

Interest rate dynamics are a key bullish signal. The positively sloped Yield Curve (10Y-3M at 0.80) and the 10Y Yield at 4.38, while neutral in isolation, when combined with strong NFP, suggest market expectations of continued economic strength. The bullish DXY at 98.21 indicates a relatively stronger US dollar, potentially influenced by these positive macro fundamentals.

The overall macro score of 67/100 solidifies a BULLISH outlook. The current environment is conducive for risk assets as it balances growth with moderating inflation, providing a stable backdrop for investment. Investors can anticipate continued economic expansion without immediate concerns of aggressive Fed tightening due to inflation, although vigilance on inflation trends remains prudent.
