Macro Assessment

The US macro picture is mixed. Positive and negative signals are roughly balanced – elevated selectivity is warranted.

Growth (Real GDP +2.0%) and inflation (CPI 4.1%) are sending mixed signals – the macroeconomic regime remains unclear.

Core CPI (3.0%) is below Headline CPI (4.1%) – energy or food prices are currently driving overall inflation. The Fed views this as a temporary effect.

The labor market is gradually cooling: Unemployment 4.3%, NFP +115k. From the Fed's perspective, this cooling is welcome.

The yield curve (10Y–3M: +0.85%) has normalized – a positive sign for the economic outlook.

VIX at 18.8 shows elevated nervousness – hedging demand in the market is rising. A weak dollar (DXY 98.4) benefits international corporate earnings, commodities and emerging markets.
