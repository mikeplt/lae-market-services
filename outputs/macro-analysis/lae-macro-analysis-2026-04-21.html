Macro Assessment

The US macro picture is currently constructive. The majority of key indicators are sending positive signals – an environment that broadly supports risk assets.

Moderate growth (Real GDP +2.3%) with still-elevated inflation (CPI 3.5%) – the disinflation phase is underway but not yet complete. The Fed is waiting for additional data points before easing monetary policy.

Core CPI (2.8%) is below Headline CPI (3.5%) – energy or food prices are currently driving overall inflation. The Fed views this as a temporary effect as long as core inflation remains moderate.

The labor market is gradually cooling: Unemployment 4.3%, NFP +178k. From the Fed's perspective, this cooling is welcome, as softening demand helps reduce inflationary pressure.

The yield curve (10Y–3M: +0.69%) has normalized – a positive sign for the economic outlook.

VIX at 19.5 shows elevated nervousness – hedging demand in the market is rising. A weak dollar (DXY 98.3) benefits international corporate earnings, commodities and emerging markets.
