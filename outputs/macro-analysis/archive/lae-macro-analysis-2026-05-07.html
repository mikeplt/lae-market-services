Macro Assessment

The US macro landscape currently presents a Goldilocks regime, characterized by moderate growth and stable inflation. Consumer Price Index (CPI) year-over-year remains at 3.49%, with Core CPI at 2.81%, both holding a neutral stance and indicating persistent but non-accelerating inflation.

Economic growth is steady, with Real GDP showing a 2.00% quarter-over-quarter increase, also neutral. This suggests the economy is expanding at a sustainable pace without signs of overheating or significant slowdown. The Unemployment Rate at 4.30% is also neutral, reinforcing the picture of a stable labor market.

Positively, employment data signals strength, with Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) monthly change at 178,000, a bullish indicator. This contrasts with the VIX, or volatility index, at 17.25, which is neutral and suggests investors are not pricing in excessive market turmoil.

The yield curve, specifically the 10-year minus 3-month spread, is 0.74, a bullish signal. This steepening often anticipates economic expansion. The 10-year yield itself at 4.33% is neutral, reflecting current interest rate expectations. The Dollar Index (DXY) at 97.86 is bullish, implying dollar strength which can sometimes pressure risk assets.

Overall, the confluence of steady GDP, contained inflation, and robust job creation, despite a strong dollar, points towards a favorable environment for risk assets. The Goldilocks regime is underpinned by manageable inflation allowing for potential sustained growth.
