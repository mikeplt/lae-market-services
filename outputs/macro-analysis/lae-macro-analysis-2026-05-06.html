Macro Assessment

The current US macroeconomic landscape presents a nuanced picture, leaning towards a cautiously optimistic environment. Inflation, as measured by CPI YoY at 3.49% and Core CPI YoY at 2.81%, remains within a neutral band, suggesting neither significant overheating nor rapid disinflationary pressures. This stability is crucial as it provides room for policy flexibility.

Economic growth is holding steady, with Real GDP QoQ at a 2.00% pace. This is a neutral indicator, signifying moderate expansion that avoids overheating or contraction. The labor market also appears robust, with the Unemployment Rate at 4.30% and Non-Farm Payrolls adding a healthy 178.00 jobs, both considered bullish signals, indicating continued job creation and a functioning economy.

A key driver of the bullish sentiment is the yield curve, with the 10Y-3M spread at a positive 0.75%, signaling bullish economic expectations. While the 10Y yield is at a neutral 4.35%, the steepening curve suggests investors anticipate future growth and potentially higher rates, though not aggressively so. The VIX at 17.25 is also neutral, indicating subdued market volatility.

The US Dollar Index (DXY) at 98.02 shows a bullish trend, which can have implications for trade and international investment. Overall, the combination of moderate inflation, solid growth, and a strengthening labor market points towards a Goldilocks regime. This environment is generally supportive of risk assets, as it offers a balance of economic expansion without excessive inflation or recessionary fears.
